Salesforce AI Agent POC:

A White Paper on Bridging Gaps in Patient Communication

YEAR: Currently working on the POC

Here is the brief overview of the white paper proposed to the client:

Objective

To evaluate if an AI-driven solution to dynamically generate context specific, patient-friendly benefit explanations ("blurbs"), aiming to reduce therapy delays (even today this is part of manual process), decrease operational costs (as a user would have to figure out and fill in the explanation before its sent out to stakeholders), and improve the patient experience by eliminating a critical administrative bottleneck.

The Challenge

The manual process of crafting blurbs for the Summary of Benefits (SOB) presented a significant obstacle to value-based care.

  • Process Inefficiency: The manual workflow was time-intensive, error-prone, and created bottlenecks that directly delayed patient access to therapy.
  • Patient Confusion: Organizations often used generic, high-level blurbs that lacked specificity, leading to patient confusion, frustration, and increased support inquiries.
  • Scalability Issues: Manually crafting tailored blurbs for every possible combination of benefits and prior authorization variables was impractical and unsustainable.

The Solution

I began researching state-of-the-art AI techniques for complex, fact-based problem-solving. My review of several technical papers, including research on Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting and the use of knowledge graphs, sparked the core idea for a solution. I theorized that this combination was a perfect fit: CoT could provide the logical, step-by-step reasoning needed to navigate complex benefit rules, while a knowledge graph could supply the structured, factual data required for accuracy. With this conceptual framework in mind, I then investigated our existing enterprise technology stack. After a thorough review of the documentation for Salesforce Agent Force, I confirmed that its underlying architecture was built on these very same principles. This was a critical realization—it meant we didn't need to build a new system from scratch. I am currently working with our team to conduct a POC and identify a feasible approach to this problem.

Benefits & Business Impacts

The proposed LLM-powered Blurb Management module delivers transformative benefits by automating blurb generation, a critical yet inefficient step in the benefits verification (BV) process.

Accelerated Patient Access to Therapy

Impact: Automating blurb generation eliminates manual peer reviews, which currently average 5 minutes per case. For an organization processing 10,000 BVs monthly where 60% require manual blurb generation, this saves 500 staff hours per month.

Value: Faster BV completion reduces time-to-therapy, enabling patients to start treatment sooner. Industry studies suggest a 1-day reduction in therapy delays can improve patient adherence by up to 5%, potentially reducing downstream complications.

Strategic Alignment: Timely therapy access supports value-based care goals by prioritizing patient outcomes and reducing care delivery delays.

Improved Patient and Provider Experience

Impact: Tailored, patient-friendly blurbs replace generic explanations, reducing confusion. Clear information (e.g., “Your plan covers the drug with a $2,000 deductible, $500 met”) can decrease patient support calls by an estimated 20–30%.

Value: Enhanced clarity minimizes provider frustration with inconsistent cost-share grids, reducing rework and improving trust. Satisfied patients and providers are more likely to engage with healthcare services.

Strategic Alignment: Patient-centric communication fosters trust and aligns with value-based care’s focus on transparency and engagement.

Cost Saving & Operational Efficiency

Impact: Automation reduces manual labor costs. Assuming an average hourly staff cost of $30, the 500 hours saved monthly translates to $15,000 in monthly savings (or ~$180,000 annually) for the 6,000 BVs that need manual intervention.

Value: The module’s scalability minimizes the need for additional staffing as volume grows. Error reduction also decreases costly rework, estimated at $5–$10 per erroneous BV.

Strategic Alignment: Cost efficiencies free up resources for high-impact, patient-focused initiatives, enhancing operational agility.

Data-Driven Insights for Continuous Improvement

Impact: The module’s metadata (e.g., reasoning logs) enables auditing and performance tracking, identifying patterns in delays or errors. This data can inform process optimizations, reducing turnaround time by an additional 10–15%.

Value: Insights from blurb generation patterns allow proactive engagement with payers to streamline coverage policies, potentially reducing denials by 5–10%.

Strategic Alignment: Leveraging data for process improvement positions the organization as a forward-thinking leader in value-based care delivery.
